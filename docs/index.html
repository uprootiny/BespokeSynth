The Central Idea

A synthesizer is a dynamical system on a space. The space has topology. The topology determines which resonances exist. If you change the topology, you change the sound.

This is not metaphorical. The eigenvalues of the Hodge Laplacian on a simplicial complex ARE the resonant frequencies. The eigenvectors ARE the mode shapes. The Betti numbers count how many independent resonances exist at each dimension.

Prerequisites

You need:

  • Comfort with linear algebra (matrices, eigenvalues, rank)
  • Basic topology (what a simplex is, what a boundary map does)
  • Familiarity with digital audio (sample rate, buffers, delay lines)

You do NOT need:

  • Category theory (we stay concrete)
  • Differential forms (we use simplicial, not de Rham cohomology)
  • Measure theory (all our spaces are finite)
